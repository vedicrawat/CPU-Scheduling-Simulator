CPU Scheduling

Scheduling Algorithms Visualized & Explained

Explore different CPU scheduling algorithms through interactive visualizations and detailed explanations that bring theory to life.

FCFS

Non-preemptive

First-Come, First-Served

Processes are executed in the order they arrive in the ready queue, ensuring fairness but potentially leading to the convoy effect.

Learn more

SJF

Optimal

Shortest Job First

Prioritizes processes with the shortest CPU burst time, minimizing average waiting time but requiring advance knowledge of burst times.

Learn more

Round Robin

Preemptive

Time-sliced Scheduling

Each process gets a small unit of CPU time in a cyclic order, providing excellent response time for interactive systems and preventing starvation.

Learn more

Priority

Configurable

Priority-based Scheduling

Processes are executed based on priority levels, with higher priority processes executing first. Can be implemented as both preemptive and non-preemptive.

Learn more
